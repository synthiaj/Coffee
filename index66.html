• Born in:Italy, the 17th Century
  • Meaning: Caffe Latte, or just “Latte” translates to “coffee with milk”.
  • Mix: A cappuccino is traditionally prepared with 1/3 espresso, 1/3 hot milk, and 1/3 steamed milk foam.
  • Facts: The term caffè latte was first used in English in 1867 by William Dean Howells in his essay "Italian Journeys". A caffè latte differs from a latte macchiato. In a latte macchiato, espresso is added to milk, rather than the reverse, and caffè lattes have a stronger coffee flavour. Ideally it is served with latte art on top.
