• Born in:Vienna, 1805
  • Meaning: The coffee beverage has its name not from the hood but from the color of the hooded robes worn by monks and nuns of the capuchin order.
  • Mix: A cappuccino is traditionally prepared with 1/3 espresso, 1/3 hot milk, and 1/3 steamed milk foam.
  • Facts: 'Cappuccino' comes from Latin Caputium, later borrowed from German/Austrian and modified into 'kapuziner'. The diminutive form of cappuccino in Italian means 'hood' or something that covers the head, thus 'cappuccino' reads 'small capuchin'.
