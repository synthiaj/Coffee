• Born in:Australia, 1980
  • Meaning: Fraser McInnes coined the term “flat white” to describe an incorrectly frothed cappuccino.
  • Mix: A flat white is an espresso based coffee beverage. The beverage is prepared by pouring microfoam (steamed milk with a glossy or velvety consistency) over a double shot of espresso.
  • Facts: The first documented appearance of the flat white is at Moors Espresso Bar in Sydney, where Alan Preston added the beverage to his permanent menu in 1985. There is also a claim that the flat white originated in Wellington, New Zealand. This claim states that the flat white was invented as a result of a "failed cappuccino" at Cafe Bodega on Willis St in 1989. It should be served in a tulip cup.
